The future of recycling is unstable

Inconsistent standards, practices and profitability are creating major challenges in recycling. If we alter the way we think about waste from a burden to a resource, we can create dramatic change. Recycling can work if we do, too.

The Way Forward

It may seem overwhelming, but start now. Prioritizing and communicating new ways of thinking about the recycling—prevention first, then reduction, reuse, repurpose, recycle/compost, incinerate/waste-to-energy, then landfill as a last resort—helps to create a holistic systems overhaul. Many cities are piloting incredibly innovative programs right now.
